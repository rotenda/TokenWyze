The Tokenisation Cryptos You Should Know: A Plain-English Guide to the Projects Reshaping Finance

Not all cryptocurrencies are speculation. A growing number of projects are building the infrastructure that will power tokenised capital markets. Here is what each one does and why it matters.

March 2026 10 min read RWA · Tokenisation · Crypto · Education
This article is for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial or investment advice. Crypto assets carry significant risk. Always consult a professional advisor before making investment decisions.

The total value of tokenised real-world assets on blockchain networks reached approximately 45 billion US dollars in early 2026. Behind that number is a set of crypto projects each playing a specific role in making tokenisation work. This is a plain-English breakdown of the most important ones.

When people talk about tokenisation, they often focus on the assets being tokenised: government bonds, real estate, gold, private credit. What gets less attention is the infrastructure that makes tokenisation possible. You need networks to settle transactions, oracles to bring real-world data on-chain, protocols to issue and manage tokenised products, and platforms to connect buyers and sellers.

The projects below sit at different points in that infrastructure. Some build the pipes. Some build the products. Some build the bridges between the two. Understanding what each one does helps you make sense of why tokenisation is more than just a concept and is already functioning at meaningful scale.

The Infrastructure Layer

These are the projects that make tokenised markets technically possible. They do not issue tokenised assets themselves, but without them, almost nothing else in this list could function reliably.

LINK
Chainlink
Oracle Network

What it does: Chainlink is a decentralised oracle network. An oracle is essentially a bridge between the blockchain and the real world. Blockchains cannot natively access external information such as the price of gold, the yield on a US Treasury bill, or whether a particular company is solvent. Chainlink retrieves that information from the real world and delivers it to smart contracts on-chain in a way that is verified and tamper-resistant.

Why it matters for tokenisation: Every tokenised asset requires accurate, real-time data about its underlying value. A tokenised government bond needs verified interest rate data. A tokenised property fund needs a reliable net asset value feed. Chainlink provides these data feeds, as well as its Proof of Reserve service, which verifies that the assets backing a token actually exist. Major institutions using Chainlink's infrastructure include banks and asset managers that have built tokenised Treasury and fund products on top of it.

Think of it as: The data verification layer that keeps tokenised markets honest.

XRP
XRP Ledger
Settlement Network

What it does: XRP is the native currency of the XRP Ledger, a blockchain built specifically for fast, low-cost settlement of financial transactions. It can process around 1,500 transactions per second at minimal cost, making it one of the fastest and cheapest settlement networks available. The ledger supports permissioned token issuance and built-in compliance metadata, features that are important for regulated financial instruments.

Why it matters for tokenisation: Several major institutions have chosen the XRP Ledger as the network for tokenised assets. Ondo Finance launched its tokenised US Treasury product on XRPL. Mercado Bitcoin, Latin America's largest crypto exchange, tokenised over 200 million dollars in fixed-income and equity assets on the ledger. Guggenheim Partners announced tokenised commercial paper offerings there. The network has become a preferred destination for institutions entering the tokenisation space precisely because it was designed for financial settlement from the start, not retrofitted for it.

Think of it as: A financial settlement rail purpose-built for institutional tokenised assets.

XLM
Stellar
Cross-Border Asset Network

What it does: Stellar is a blockchain network designed for fast, low-cost cross-border payments and asset tokenisation. It processes around 1,000 transactions per second with very low fees and has a native framework for KYC and compliance built into the protocol itself. This makes it particularly attractive for tokenised assets that need to move across borders while remaining compliant with local regulations.

Why it matters for tokenisation: Stellar has become a significant network for regulated tokenised products. Franklin Templeton launched its Benji tokenised money market fund on Stellar, the first tokenised fund of its kind on a public blockchain. WisdomTree Prime built tokenised investment funds there as well. The network hosted over 590 million dollars in tokenised assets by mid-2025, with partnerships involving Paxos, Ondo, and Societe Generale Forge pushing that figure higher. For South Africa specifically, Stellar's cross-border capabilities and built-in compliance tools make it relevant for future tokenised capital flows within the SADC region.

Think of it as: A compliance-first network for cross-border tokenised assets.

The Product Layer

These projects go beyond infrastructure and actually create tokenised financial products that investors can hold and use.

ONDO
Ondo Finance
Tokenised Securities Protocol

What it does: Ondo Finance builds tokenised financial products, primarily focused on US government securities and money market instruments. Its flagship product, OUSG, gives qualified investors on-chain exposure to short-term US Treasuries. Ondo combines an asset management arm that creates the tokenised products with a technology arm that builds DeFi protocols to make those products accessible and composable.

Why it matters for tokenisation: Ondo is one of the clearest examples of tokenisation moving from concept to working product. It brings traditional fixed-income returns onto blockchain rails, meaning investors can earn yield from US government bonds without going through a traditional broker. In 2025, Ondo announced plans to expand into tokenised stocks and ETFs on Solana, allowing users to buy and sell traditional securities using standard crypto wallets. The project has attracted serious institutional attention because it focuses on regulated, compliant offerings rather than unregulated speculation.

Think of it as: A bridge that brings government bonds and equities onto the blockchain.

PAXG
PAX Gold
Tokenised Commodity

What it does: PAX Gold is a token where each coin represents one troy ounce of physical gold held in vaults in London by Paxos Trust Company, a regulated financial institution. Holders of PAXG own actual allocated gold, not a derivative or a promise. The gold can be redeemed for physical delivery or sold back to Paxos at any time.

Why it matters for tokenisation: PAXG is one of the most straightforward demonstrations of what tokenisation means in practice. Gold is a globally recognised store of value, but owning physical gold has traditionally meant dealing with storage, insurance, and logistics. PAXG eliminates all of that. You can buy a fraction of an ounce, transfer it instantly anywhere in the world, use it as collateral in DeFi protocols, or sell it 24 hours a day. For South Africa, where gold is deeply embedded in the economy, tokenised gold represents a particularly relevant use case for democratising access to a commodity that has historically required significant capital to hold directly.

Think of it as: Physical gold in your crypto wallet.

CFG
Centrifuge
Private Credit Tokenisation

What it does: Centrifuge is a protocol that enables the tokenisation of real-world credit assets, primarily private loans, invoices, and other forms of private debt. It allows businesses to bring their real-world assets on-chain as collateral to access DeFi liquidity, while giving investors exposure to yield-generating credit products that were previously only available to institutional investors.

Why it matters for tokenisation: Private credit is one of the largest and least accessible asset classes in traditional finance. Centrifuge opens it up by tokenising the underlying loans and making them available to a broader pool of capital. It has become an institutional toolkit for tokenised funds and on-chain asset management, attracting attention from DeFi protocols and traditional lenders alike. For emerging markets like South Africa, the ability to tokenise trade finance and private credit could unlock significant new sources of capital for businesses that struggle to access traditional lending.

Think of it as: A platform that puts private loans and credit on the blockchain.

The Interoperability Layer

Tokenised assets do not all live on the same blockchain. Moving value between different networks securely is one of the hardest problems in the space. These projects work on solving it.

AVAX
Avalanche
Institutional Blockchain Platform

What it does: Avalanche is a blockchain platform that allows organisations to create their own customised blockchain networks, called subnets, that operate within the Avalanche ecosystem. Each subnet can have its own rules, validators, and compliance requirements, making it possible to build a private, permissioned blockchain for institutional use while still benefiting from Avalanche's underlying infrastructure and interoperability.

Why it matters for tokenisation: Avalanche has become a preferred platform for financial institutions wanting to build their own tokenised asset infrastructure. JPMorgan, Citi, and T. Rowe Price have all conducted tokenised asset experiments using Avalanche's subnet architecture. The ability to create a compliance-controlled environment while remaining connected to the broader Avalanche network makes it attractive for regulated markets. Its speed, processing thousands of transactions per second with sub-second finality, is also well suited to capital markets applications.

Think of it as: A platform that lets banks and institutions build their own compliant blockchains.

DOT
Polkadot
Cross-Chain Interoperability

What it does: Polkadot is a blockchain that connects multiple specialised blockchains into a single unified network. It allows different blockchains to communicate and share information with each other securely, solving one of the fundamental problems in crypto: assets and data trapped in isolated networks that cannot interact.

Why it matters for tokenisation: As tokenised assets proliferate across different blockchains, the ability to move them between networks becomes critical. A tokenised bond issued on one chain needs to be usable as collateral on another. A tokenised property token should be tradeable on multiple platforms. Polkadot's interoperability architecture is designed to make this possible without compromising security. For the future of tokenised capital markets, where no single blockchain will host everything, cross-chain infrastructure like Polkadot's becomes part of the essential plumbing.

Think of it as: The network that connects all the other tokenisation networks together.

The Bigger Picture

These are not speculative bets on technology that might one day be useful. They are infrastructure projects serving a market that is already 45 billion dollars in size and growing rapidly.

What is striking about the tokenisation crypto landscape in 2026 is how clearly the roles have become defined. There is infrastructure for data verification (Chainlink), infrastructure for settlement (XRP, Stellar), platforms for building institutional networks (Avalanche), protocols for issuing tokenised products (Ondo, Centrifuge), tokenised commodities you can hold directly (PAX Gold), and networks for connecting it all together (Polkadot).

For South Africa, each of these projects is relevant in a different way. Chainlink's oracle infrastructure could support verified data feeds for JSE-listed tokenised securities. Stellar's cross-border capabilities align with the SADC region's need for affordable cross-border payment and settlement infrastructure. Ondo's model of tokenised Treasuries could be adapted for tokenised South African government bonds. PAX Gold's approach to commodity tokenisation is directly applicable to South Africa's gold and platinum mining sectors.

The institutions and regulators that understand what each of these projects does, and why, will be far better positioned to design a tokenisation framework that actually works for South Africa's financial markets. That understanding starts here.
